Project Brief: DSY Teahouse
1. Project Overview
Objective: To design and develop a lightweight, static informational website for DSY Teahouse. The site will serve as a digital storefront, conveying the unique ambiance of the physical space and introducing visitors to the rich traditions of Chinese-Japanese fusion tea culture.
Technology Stack: HTML5, CSS3, Vanilla JavaScript.
Core Vibe: Tranquil, historical, grounded, and inviting.

2. Design & Aesthetic Guidelines
The visual language should transport the user into an old tatami room the moment the page loads, balancing ancient tradition with clean, modern web usability.

Color Palette: Earthy, natural tones inspired by the physical elements of the teahouse.

Primary: Washi Paper White (#F5F2EB) and Tatami Straw (#DED2A8).

Secondary: Deep Matcha Green (#4A5D23) and Aged Teapot Clay (#8B5A2B).

Accents: Faded Ink Black (#2C2C2C) for text.

Typography: * Headings: An elegant serif font (like Playfair Display or Noto Serif JP/TC) that evokes the feeling of traditional brush strokes or old print.

Body: A clean, legible sans-serif font (like Lato or Inter) to maintain readability across devices.

Visual Style & UI Elements: * Textures: Subtle, seamless background patterns of bamboo mats or rice paper.

Soft Lighting Elements: Implement soft, diffused CSS shadows (a "fuzzy, soft" lighting effect) behind content cards or images to mimic warm, dappled sunlight filtering through Shoji screens.

Minimalism: Embrace "Wabi-Sabi" (the beauty of imperfection and simplicity). Use plenty of negative space so the content breathes.

Subtle Animations: Use JavaScript/CSS to add gentle interactions, such as a slow fade-in on scroll or a subtle floating animation for steam rising from a teacup hero image.

3. Site Architecture & Content Strategy
A single-page application (SPA) with smooth scrolling, or a simple 4-page structure, works best for this scope.

Section 1: The Hero (Home)
Visual: A high-quality, atmospheric, full-width image (or subtle looping video) of the teahouse interior, focusing on the tatami mats, low wooden tables, and tea sets.

Copy: A welcoming, poetic tagline (e.g., "Where Time Slows Down. Experience the Harmony of Chinese and Japanese Tea Traditions.").

Action: A clear "Discover Our Space" call-to-action button leading to the next section.

Section 2: Our Philosophy (About)
Visual: Side-by-side layout featuring text and an image of the brewing process.

Copy: A brief narrative about DSY Teahouse. Explain the "Asian concept" fusion—how the mindful, structured Japanese Chanoyu (tea ceremony) meets the relaxed, communal Chinese Gongfu style. Emphasize the history and the sensory experience of the tatami setting.

Section 3: The Tea & The Space (Offerings)
Visual: A cleanly structured CSS Grid or Flexbox layout displaying key offerings.

Copy: * Highlight signature teas (e.g., Aged Pu'er, Ceremonial Matcha, Tieguanyin).

Describe the physical experience (e.g., private tatami rooms, meditation corners).

Section 4: Visit Us (Footer / Contact)
Visual: Minimalist layout, potentially incorporating a stylized, hand-drawn map.

Copy: * Operating hours.

Physical address and contact details.

Links to social media for ongoing updates and aesthetic photography.

4. Technical Implementation Notes
Semantic HTML: Structure the document logically (<header>, <main>, <section>, <footer>) to ensure accessibility and basic SEO out of the box.

CSS Layouts: Rely on CSS Grid for the gallery/menu sections and Flexbox for navigation and alignment. Keep the CSS modular so it's easy to maintain.

JavaScript: Keep it minimal. Use JS solely for enhancing the user experience—such as implementing a custom intersection observer for fade-in scroll effects, managing a mobile hamburger menu, or handling a simple lightbox for the gallery.

Asset Optimization: Since the aesthetic relies heavily on imagery, compress all JPEGs/WebPs to ensure the site loads quickly without losing the high-fidelity look of the teahouse.
